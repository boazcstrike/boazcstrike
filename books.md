# Books

A self-curated reading list. Community-driven canon, research-rich picks, and personal favorites across psychology, software development, economics, geopolitics, and trauma/mind-body work.

---

## Personal Favorites

- *Elephants on Acid* — Alex Boese. Personal favorite; weird, rigorous, and unforgettable history of fringe behavioral experiments.
- *The Lucifer Effect* — Phillip Zimbardo. Stanford Prison Experiment lead's full account of how good people turn evil. **so good.**
- *Has China Won?* — Kishore Mahbubani. Singaporean diplomat's frank case that the US has already lost the strategic contest if it keeps misreading Beijing. **epic read.**

## Psychology

### Cognitive & Behavioral

- *Thinking, Fast and Slow* — Daniel Kahneman. Nobel laureate's synthesis of System 1/System 2 and the heuristics-and-biases program.
- *Predictably Irrational* — Dan Ariely. Field experiments showing the systematic, predictable shape of human irrationality.
- *Drive* — Daniel Pink. Synthesis of self-determination theory; autonomy, mastery, purpose as the actual motivators.
- *Blink* — Malcolm Gladwell. Popular treatment of thin-slicing and rapid cognition research.
- *Strangers to Ourselves* — Timothy Wilson. UVA social psychologist on the adaptive unconscious and why introspection so often misses.
- *Nudge* — Richard Thaler & Cass Sunstein. Foundational behavioral econ text on choice architecture; Thaler's Nobel-winning libertarian paternalism.
- *Misbehaving* — Richard Thaler. Memoir-meets-research history of behavioral economics.
- *The Undoing Project* — Michael Lewis. Narrative biography of Kahneman & Tversky's heuristics-and-biases program.
- *Judgment Under Uncertainty: Heuristics and Biases* — Kahneman, Slovic & Tversky (eds.). Original academic anthology behind modern decision science.
- *Noise* — Kahneman, Sibony & Sunstein. Empirical treatment of variance in human judgment; complements Thinking Fast and Slow.
- *Stumbling on Happiness* — Daniel Gilbert. Harvard social psychologist on affective forecasting errors.
- *Mindware* — Richard Nisbett. Distillation of decades of cognition and reasoning research.
- *Algorithms to Live By* — Brian Christian & Tom Griffiths. CS-meets-cognitive-science on bounded rationality.
- *The Righteous Mind* — Jonathan Haidt. Moral Foundations Theory backed by extensive cross-cultural empirical work.
- *Peak* — Anders Ericsson & Robert Pool. The actual deliberate-practice research, corrected from pop-culture distortions.
- *The Little Book of Psychology* — Emily Ralls & Caroline Riggs. Quick-reference primer on the major schools and findings.

### Social Psychology

- *The Social Animal* — Elliot Aronson. The standard undergraduate social psychology text, decades and revisions deep.
- *The Person and the Situation* — Lee Ross & Richard Nisbett. The classic case for situational power over personality in predicting behavior.
- *Social Engineering: The Art of Human Hacking* — Chris Hadnagy. Field-tested human-vector attack techniques; bridges social psych and security.
- *Influence: The Psychology of Persuasion* — Robert Cialdini. Six principles grounded in field experiments; standard undergrad text.
- *Pre-Suasion* — Robert Cialdini. Follow-up focused on attentional priming.
- *Mistakes Were Made (But Not by Me)* — Carol Tavris & Elliot Aronson. Definitive popular treatment of cognitive dissonance theory.
- *The Power of Habit* — Charles Duhigg. Synthesizes MIT/Duke habit-loop neuroscience and organizational behavior research.
- *Mindset* — Carol Dweck. Stanford research on implicit theories of intelligence.
- *Quiet* — Susan Cain. Synthesis of introversion/extraversion research from Cain, Aron, and Kagan.
- *Talking to Strangers* — Malcolm Gladwell. Surveys default-to-truth and transparency research for a general audience.
- *Bowling Alone* — Robert Putnam. Harvard political scientist's data-heavy account of declining social capital.
- *The Tipping Point* — Malcolm Gladwell. Popularizes Granovetter, Milgram, and broken-windows research.

### Clinical, Trauma & Personality

- *The Body Keeps the Score* — Bessel van der Kolk. Boston trauma clinician synthesizing decades of PTSD neuroimaging and somatic research.
- *Trauma and Recovery* — Judith Herman. Harvard psychiatrist's foundational text on complex PTSD.
- *Waking the Tiger* — Peter Levine. Somatic Experiencing founder; primary source for body-based trauma therapy.
- *Complex PTSD: From Surviving to Thriving* — Pete Walker. Clinician-authored, widely recommended on r/CPTSD.
- *Cognitive Behavior Therapy: Basics and Beyond* — Judith Beck. The standard CBT training manual.
- *Feeling Good* — David Burns. Stanford psychiatrist; rare self-help with RCT evidence for bibliotherapy in depression.
- *The Noonday Demon* — Andrew Solomon. National Book Award-winning depression compendium.
- *An Unquiet Mind* — Kay Redfield Jamison. Johns Hopkins psychiatrist's memoir of bipolar disorder.
- *Personality: What Makes You the Way You Are* — Daniel Nettle. Big Five personality research distilled.
- *Without Conscience* — Robert Hare. Creator of the PCL-R psychopathy checklist; primary source on forensic psychopathy.
- *I'm OK – You're OK* — Thomas Harris. Classic transactional analysis text still on clinical reading lists.

### Neuroscience & Mind

- *The Man Who Mistook His Wife for a Hat* — Oliver Sacks. The classic clinical-essay collection on neurological case studies.
- *Behave* — Robert Sapolsky. Stanford neuroendocrinologist's 800-page integration of neuroscience, endocrinology, and behavioral biology.
- *Why Zebras Don't Get Ulcers* — Robert Sapolsky. Definitive popular text on stress physiology.
- *The Feeling of What Happens* — Antonio Damasio. Builds on his somatic marker hypothesis.
- *Descartes' Error* — Antonio Damasio. Iowa neurologist's case-study-driven argument for emotion in rational decision-making.
- *Phantoms in the Brain* — V.S. Ramachandran. UCSD neuroscientist on body image, mirror neurons, and synesthesia.
- *Incognito* — David Eagleman. Baylor neuroscientist on unconscious processing.
- *The Brain That Changes Itself* — Norman Doidge. Survey of neuroplasticity research.
- *Consciousness Explained* — Daniel Dennett. Tufts philosopher-of-mind's multiple drafts model.
- *How Emotions Are Made* — Lisa Feldman Barrett. Northeastern neuroscientist's constructed-emotion theory.
- *A Mind of Its Own* — Cordelia Fine. Critical synthesis of self-deception and motivated cognition research.

### Evolutionary, Developmental & Dark Side

- *The Blank Slate* — Steven Pinker. Harvard evolutionary psychologist on innate cognition vs. social construction.
- *How the Mind Works* — Steven Pinker. Comprehensive evolutionary-cognitive synthesis.
- *The Better Angels of Our Nature* — Steven Pinker. Data-heavy account of declining violence.
- *The Moral Animal* — Robert Wright. Accessible introduction to evolutionary psychology.
- *Sapiens* — Yuval Noah Harari. Cross-disciplinary evolutionary and cognitive history.
- *The Selfish Gene* — Richard Dawkins. Foundational gene-centered view underpinning modern evolutionary psychology.
- *The Gardener and the Carpenter* — Alison Gopnik. Berkeley developmental psychologist's research-grounded counter to "parenting" culture.
- *The Philosophical Baby* — Alison Gopnik. Survey of infant cognition research.
- *Snakes in Suits* — Paul Babiak & Robert Hare. PCL-R framework applied to corporate psychopathy.
- *The Sociopath Next Door* — Martha Stout. Harvard Medical School clinician on subclinical antisocial personality.
- *Mistakes and Murderers* — David Canter. UK investigative-psychology pioneer on offender profiling.
- *The Anatomy of Evil* — Michael Stone. Columbia forensic psychiatrist's empirical typology of violent offenders.

## Software Development

### Foundations & Craft

- *The Pragmatic Programmer* — Hunt & Thomas. The canonical "how to think like a software craftsman" book.
- *Structure and Interpretation of Computer Programs (SICP)* — Abelson & Sussman. The MIT 6.001 classic.
- *The Art of Computer Programming* — Donald Knuth. The definitive multi-volume reference on algorithms.
- *Introduction to Algorithms (CLRS)* — Cormen, Leiserson, Rivest, Stein. The default algorithms textbook worldwide.
- *Code: The Hidden Language of Computer Hardware and Software* — Charles Petzold. Clearest bottom-up explanation of how computers actually work.
- *Computer Systems: A Programmer's Perspective (CSAPP)* — Bryant & O'Hallaron. Connects C, assembly, memory, and OS into one mental model.
- *The Mythical Man-Month* — Fred Brooks. Decades-old essays on software project realities; still cited weekly on HN.

### Software Engineering & Practice

- *Clean Code* — Robert C. Martin. Polarizing but pervasive lingua franca for naming, function size, and code smells.
- *Code Complete* — Steve McConnell. The 900-page encyclopedia of construction practices.
- *Working Effectively with Legacy Code* — Michael Feathers. Definitive guide for taming untested, tangled codebases.
- *A Philosophy of Software Design* — John Ousterhout. Stanford CS190's "deep modules" thesis; modern counterpoint to Clean Code.
- *The Software Engineer's Guidebook* — Gergely Orosz. Modern community favorite; junior-to-staff arc.
- *Software Engineering at Google* — Titus Winters et al. Google's public articulation of large-scale engineering.
- *Accelerate* — Forsgren, Humble & Kim. The DORA research distilled; empirical case for the four key delivery metrics.

### System Design & Distributed Systems

- *Designing Data-Intensive Applications (DDIA)* — Martin Kleppmann. Most-recommended modern book on storage, replication, and stream processing.
- *System Design Interview, Vol. 1 & 2* — Alex Xu. De facto FAANG-style design interview prep canon.
- *Building Microservices* — Sam Newman. Reference text for service decomposition.
- *Site Reliability Engineering* — Beyer, Jones, Petoff, Murphy. Google's free SRE book; defines SLOs and error budgets for the industry.
- *Release It!* — Michael Nygard. Stability patterns (circuit breaker, bulkhead) that became standard distributed-systems vocabulary.
- *Understanding Distributed Systems* — Roberto Vitillo. Concise modern primer.

### Languages & Paradigms

- *The Go Programming Language* — Donovan & Kernighan. Canonical Go book; co-authored by K of K&R.
- *Programming Rust* — Blandy, Orendorff & Tindall. Most-cited Rust book after the official Rust Book.
- *Rust for Rustaceans* — Jon Gjengset. Intermediate-to-advanced Rust standard.
- *Fluent Python* — Luciano Ramalho. Deep-Python reference.
- *Effective Python* — Brett Slatkin. 90 specific items every Python engineer is expected to internalize.
- *JavaScript: The Good Parts* — Douglas Crockford. Short, opinionated, historically the entry point to taking JS seriously.
- *You Don't Know JS Yet* — Kyle Simpson. The deep-dive series for really learning JavaScript.
- *Effective TypeScript* — Dan Vanderkam. 83 items the TS community treats as the standard idioms reference.
- *Effective Modern C++* — Scott Meyers. C++11/14 idioms book.
- *A Tour of C++* — Bjarne Stroustrup. The creator's compact tour; the recommended modern C++ entry point.
- *The C Programming Language (K&R)* — Kernighan & Ritchie. The original; still the model of how a language book should read.

### Architecture, DDD & Refactoring

- *Refactoring* — Martin Fowler. Vocabulary of code transformations.
- *Domain-Driven Design* — Eric Evans. The "blue book" that gave us bounded contexts, aggregates, and ubiquitous language.
- *Implementing Domain-Driven Design* — Vaughn Vernon. The "red book" that operationalizes Evans for working teams.
- *Patterns of Enterprise Application Architecture* — Martin Fowler. Pattern catalog (Repository, Unit of Work, etc.).
- *Design Patterns* — Gamma, Helm, Johnson, Vlissides. The Gang of Four book that defined OO design vocabulary.
- *Fundamentals of Software Architecture* — Mark Richards & Neal Ford. Current standard architecture textbook for staff-track engineers.

### Performance, Databases & Reliability

- *Systems Performance* — Brendan Gregg. Definitive guide to Linux/Unix performance methodology and tooling.
- *Database Internals* — Alex Petrov. Most-cited book for understanding how storage engines and distributed databases are built.
- *High Performance MySQL* — Schwartz, Zaitsev, Tkachenko. Long-standing reference for query tuning, replication, and operational MySQL.
- *The Linux Programming Interface* — Michael Kerrisk. Exhaustive syscalls/Linux reference.

### Security

- *The Web Application Hacker's Handbook* — Stuttard & Pinto. Canonical web pentesting/AppSec reference.
- *Cryptography Engineering* — Ferguson, Schneier & Kohno. Pragmatic crypto book Schneier himself recommends.
- *Serious Cryptography* — Jean-Philippe Aumasson. Modern, accessible follow-up that's become the new community default.

### ML / AI Engineering

- *Designing Machine Learning Systems* — Chip Huyen. Reference for productionizing ML — feature stores, monitoring, retraining loops.
- *Hands-On Machine Learning with Scikit-Learn, Keras & TensorFlow* — Aurélien Géron. Most-recommended applied-ML book.
- *Deep Learning* — Goodfellow, Bengio & Courville. Foundational DL textbook ("the flower book").
- *AI Engineering* — Chip Huyen. 2025 standard for building applications on top of foundation models — RAG, evals, inference.

### Career & Mindset

- *The Staff Engineer's Path* — Tanya Reilly. Defining book on the senior-individual-contributor track.
- *Staff Engineer: Leadership Beyond the Management Track* — Will Larson. Companion canon to Reilly.
- *An Elegant Puzzle* — Will Larson. Widely-cited reference on engineering management systems thinking.
- *The Manager's Path* — Camille Fournier. Standard guidebook for the IC-to-manager transition.
- *The Phoenix Project* — Kim, Behr & Spafford. The DevOps novel that made flow, WIP, and constraints legible.

## Economics & Political Philosophy

- *Anatomy of the State* — Murray Rothbard. Concise dismantling of the state as a parasitic institution; Rothbardian classic.
- *Democracy: The God That Failed* — Hans-Hermann Hoppe. Provocative argument that democracy is structurally worse than monarchy on time-preference grounds.
- *Chaos Theory* — Robert P. Murphy. Short, lucid case for private law and anarcho-capitalist legal order.
- *Spontaneous Order* — James Rachels (and the broader spontaneous-order tradition). Order as emergent rather than designed.
- *Human Action* — Ludwig von Mises. Magnum opus of praxeology and the foundational treatise of modern Austrian economics.
- *Bureaucracy* — Ludwig von Mises. Surgical demolition of why bureaucratic management cannot replicate market calculation.
- *The Road to Serfdom* — F.A. Hayek. Classic warning that central planning erodes the institutions that make freedom possible.
- *The Constitution of Liberty* — F.A. Hayek. Hayek's mature, systematic defense of a rules-based liberal order.
- *The Law* — Frédéric Bastiat. 1850 pamphlet that still frames the legitimate scope of law more clearly than most modern political theory.
- *Capitalism and Freedom* — Milton Friedman. Accessible case for economic freedom as a precondition for political freedom.
- *Free to Choose* — Milton & Rose Friedman. Practical companion to *Capitalism and Freedom*.
- *Basic Economics* — Thomas Sowell. No-jargon introduction that trains tradeoffs-and-incentives thinking.
- *Knowledge and Decisions* — Thomas Sowell. Sowell's most ambitious work, extending Hayek's knowledge problem.
- *Economics in One Lesson* — Henry Hazlitt. The seen-vs-unseen lesson applied to common policy fallacies.
- *Antifragile* — Nassim Nicholas Taleb. Argues for systems that gain from disorder.
- *Skin in the Game* — Nassim Nicholas Taleb. On asymmetry, accountability, and bearing the downside of one's own decisions.
- *Debt: The First 5,000 Years* — David Graeber. Anthropological counter-canon that challenges the Austrian/neoclassical money origin story.
- *Capital in the Twenty-First Century* — Thomas Piketty. Signature contemporary case for r > g and rising wealth concentration; useful steelman.
- *Why Nations Fail* — Daron Acemoglu & James Robinson. Institutions-over-geography thesis from the eventual Nobel laureates.

## Geopolitics & China

- *Has China Won?* — Kishore Mahbubani. Singaporean diplomat's frank case that the US has already lost the strategic contest if it keeps misreading Beijing.
- *Destined for War* — Graham Allison. Coined "Thucydides Trap"; 16 historical case studies of rising vs ruling powers.
- *On China* — Henry Kissinger. Insider account of US-China rapprochement and the cultural-strategic logic of Chinese statecraft.
- *The Silk Roads* — Peter Frankopan. Reframes world history with Asia and the trade corridors at the center.
- *The New Silk Roads* — Peter Frankopan. Sequel focused on the present-day Eurasian pivot.
- *Prisoners of Geography* — Tim Marshall. Ten maps that explain why states behave as they do.
- *The Hundred-Year Marathon* — Michael Pillsbury. Hawkish but heavily-sourced argument that Beijing has a long-horizon strategy to displace US primacy.
- *China's Good War* — Rana Mitter. How the CCP repurposes WWII memory to manufacture legitimacy and nationalism.
- *The Avoidable War* — Kevin Rudd. Former Australian PM lays out a "managed strategic competition" framework grounded in Xi Jinping Thought.
- *The Rise and Fall of the Great Powers* — Paul Kennedy. Classic on imperial overstretch.
- *World Order* — Henry Kissinger. Tour of competing civilizational concepts of order.
- *Superpower Showdown* — Bob Davis & Lingling Wei. Reportorial blow-by-blow of the US-China trade war.

## Health, Trauma & Mind-Body

- *Scattered Minds* — Gabor Maté. Maté's account of ADHD as a developmental, attachment-rooted phenomenon.
- *The Myth of Normal: Trauma, Illness and Healing in a Toxic Culture* — Gabor Maté. Maté's late-career synthesis on how a toxic culture manufactures illness.
- *When the Body Says No: The Cost of Hidden Stress* — Gabor Maté. Clinical argument that suppressed emotion drives chronic disease.
- *In the Realm of Hungry Ghosts* — Gabor Maté. Clinical and humane account of addiction as an attempt to soothe unmet developmental need.
- *Hold On to Your Kids* — Gabor Maté & Gordon Neufeld. Attachment-first parenting argument.
- *The Body Keeps the Score* — Bessel van der Kolk. Reference text on how trauma reshapes brain, body, and behavior.
- *Waking the Tiger* — Peter Levine. Origin text of Somatic Experiencing.
- *In an Unspoken Voice* — Peter Levine. Levine's deeper, more clinical follow-up integrating polyvagal theory with somatic practice.
- *The Polyvagal Theory* — Stephen Porges. Primary source for the autonomic-states model now underpinning most trauma-informed care.
- *Why Zebras Don't Get Ulcers* — Robert Sapolsky. Stanford neuroendocrinologist on how chronic stress wrecks human physiology.
- *Behave* — Robert Sapolsky. Sweeping multi-level account of why humans do what they do.
- *Trauma and Recovery* — Judith Herman. Established complex PTSD as a clinical category; defines the three-stage recovery model.
- *Sensorimotor Psychotherapy* — Pat Ogden & Janina Fisher. Clinician-grade integration of attachment, neuroscience, and body-based intervention.
- *Healing the Fragmented Selves of Trauma Survivors* — Janina Fisher. Practical parts-work approach to dissociation in complex trauma.
- *My Grandmother's Hands* — Resmaa Menakem. Somatic frame for racialized and intergenerational trauma.
